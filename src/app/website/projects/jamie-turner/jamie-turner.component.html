Industry: Jewerly

American-made luxury

UX Strategy | WEBSITE DESIGN

Jamie Turner is a fine jewelry brand based in Austin, Texas, Rooted in family heritage, natural gemstones, and meticulous craftsmanship.

Current website: functional but transactional, lacking emotional depth.

Goal: Transform the homepage into an aspirational brand experience that inspires trust and desire before purchase.

See The Live Page

Project goals

1. Elevate brand perception: positioning Jamie Turner as a modern expression of Texas luxury, grounded in craftsmanship and elegance.

2. Celebrate individuality: emphasizing that each piece is one of a kind, designed to reflect the unique style and personality of the person who wears it.

3. Build emotional connection: create trust and desire through meaning and beauty before any thought of price.

4. Highlight story and values: share the family legacy, natural gemstones, and the artistry of the Austin studio.

5. Encourage exploration: lead visitors through a considered and aspirational brand experience.

6. Drive Qualified interest: from exploring the product catalog to in-person experiences such as private appointments and store visits.

Understanding what holds back the luxury experience

1. Lack of storytelling: the homepage feels transactional, missing the deeper narrative.

2. Early focus on price: pieces are shown with price tags before trust is built.

3. Underused brand heritage: family legacy, gemstones, and Austin craft are hidden in the layout.

4. Generic visual hierarchy: the design does not reflect the spaciousness, elegance, and refinement expected of a luxury brand.

5. Disconnected first impression: Opening visuals do not signal elegance, exclusivity, or meaning.

Understanding the clients who seek meaning, craftsmanship, and distinction

1. Design/Conscious: discerning clients with a refined eye for detail, quality, and aesthetics.

2. Comfortable with high-end pricing: ready to invest when value, trust, and meaning are clear.

3. Woman and men with purpose: women choosing pieces for self-expression. Men gifting jewelry for milestones and meaningful moments.

4. Rooted in the U.S. Inspired by the world: Clients who value a blend of local identity and global elegance.

UX Strategy:
from transaction to connection

1. Inspire before you sell: create a sense of wonder and aspiration before showing products.

2. Build emotional value: Highlight the people, process, and meaning behind every piece.

3. Celebrate individuality: remind clients each creation is one-of-a-kind, made for their story.

4. Stablish visual elegance: use a clean, spacious, and editorial layout to convey refinement.

5. Guide, don't rush: encourage thoughtful exploration instead of urgency.

After having clear objectives and better understanding the niche, I began to create the new structure taking into account the important points to improve.

1. Hero section

  • Large, emotionally resonant image (editorial-style or close-up of a piece)
  • Short, poetic headline (for example: “Crafted by hand. Chosen by heart.”)
  • Sub-headline introducing the values of craftsmanship and uniqueness.
  • Primary CTA: “Explore the Collection” or “Discover Our Story”

    • 2. The brand Story

  • Concise narrative introducing the brand's origin, the family connection, the passion for gemstones, and the Austin studio.
  • Supporting image (Robert and Jamie, or a process shot)
  • Supporting with a CTA: “Our story”

    • 3. CORE VALUES SECTION

    This section was meant to show what makes the precious jewels offered by Jamie Turner unique, this section had to contain three visual content blocks:

  • Natural Gemstones
  • Handcrafted in Austin
  • Designed for You

    • Each with short copy and imagery.

    4. Signature piece highlights

  • 3 to 5 hero pieces, displayed in a clean and editorial format.
  • No pricing shown
  • Story-driven microcopy and a CTA: “View the Full Collection”

    • 5. Studio or retail experience

  • Invitation to visit Neiman Marcus or schedule a private appointment.
  • Lifestyle image or studio detail shot.
  • CTA: “Find a retailer near you”

    • Results Obtained

  • Probable increase in page retention as users find what they're looking for faster.
  • Improved conversion rates toward catalogs/products thanks to visible and well-placed CTAs.
  • Improved emotional experience by connecting with brand values (artisanal, luxury, naturalness), which can foster loyalty.
  • Possible improvement in trust metrics (more time on "Our Story," more newsletter signups) thanks to the added visual legitimacy.
