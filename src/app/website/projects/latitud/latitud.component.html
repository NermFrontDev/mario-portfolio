Industry: Commercial Photography

Commercial Cold-Weather Photography

UX/UI DESIGN | WEBSITE DESIGN

Latitude 90° is a photography studio run by professional photographer MG Weber, specializing in capturing landscapes in the planet's most extreme climates, especially in glacial regions. Exclusive licenses are available for commercial use of these stunning images, ideal for brands seeking to tell powerful visual stories.

The Challenge

Was to design the website for Latitude 90°, MG Weber's commercial photography studio. The goal was to create a digital experience that reflected the visual, emotional, and narrative magnitude of the images captured by the photographer in some of the most extreme and remote locations on the planet: glaciers and polar regions.

One of the main challenges was finding the balance between the powerful visual aesthetics of the photographs and intuitive and functional navigation, especially for a professional audience looking to acquire exclusive usage rights for campaigns, editorials, and global brands. The content had to communicate not only visual beauty but also commercial value.

In short, the challenge was to translate the experience of exploring and capturing wilderness into a sophisticated, visually immersive, and commercially effective digital experience.

Design Process

Rom the beginning of the project, I knew that the visual content—the stunning photographs captured by MG Weber in extreme climates—would be the core of the entire experience. Therefore, before moving into visual or aesthetic aspects, we focused on structuring functional wireframes that would help us understand how the navigation flows and what the most logical content hierarchy would be for the user.

Main Objective: Clarity and Purpose

The first step was to define the site's main objectives:

  • Showcase the visual and commercial value of the photographs.
  • Facilitate contact for exclusive licenses or collaborations.
  • Build a visual narrative that supports the credibility of the work on the ground.

    • This led us to design a very clean information architecture, with a few key sections: Gallery, Expeditions, About MG Weber, and a section dedicated to Commercial Licensing.

    Early feedback from the client

    We shared these wireframes with photographer MG Weber to validate that the narrative and commercial approach aligned with his vision. This allowed us to fine-tune details early on, such as prioritizing certain texts over photographic technique or ensuring that each image told a story, not just a landscape.

    Key decisions

  • Use simple, high-contrast typography that supports the editorial and professional tone.
  • Integrate a mobile-first experience, thinking about users who are likely to access the site from mobile devices while traveling, on expeditions, or conducting quick visual research.
  • Design modular blocks that can scale over time: for example, a gallery that can be expanded with new expeditions without breaking the design.

    • Importance of the “Explore Our Library” section on the Latitude 90° website

    Including an "Explore Our Library" section was a key decision in the design of the Latitude 90° photography studio's website, as it serves multiple strategic functions for both the user experience and photographer MG Weber's business goals.

  • Immediate visibility of the commercial portfolio: this section functions as a curated showcase of the most striking images from the photographer's visual archive. It allows the user to quickly explore the available categories—such as Wildlife or Landscape—with simple and visually powerful navigation. Displaying featured images at the beginning creates an immediate emotional connection and demonstrates the quality of the work without having to read much text.
  • Clarity in the value proposition: the title “Discover License-Ready Images” clearly communicates that these images are not only for viewing, but are also available for commercial licensing. This eliminates friction in the browsing process: the visitor instantly understands that they can use these images in campaigns, editorials, or media, upon agreement.

    conclusions

    From the outset, this project represented a unique challenge: translating the technical rigor, commercial exclusivity, and wild beauty of glaciers into a clear, intuitive, and visually powerful digital experience. The design had to be clean and functional, yet distinctive; it had to communicate professional confidence, without losing the sense of exploration and wonder that defines the studio.

    Among the most valuable lessons learned from this project, I would highlight:

  • Designing with a narrative purpose: each section was built not only to inform, but also to guide the user through a coherent story, from the presentation of the studio to the gallery and commercial contact.
  • Balance between editorial and commercial content: ensuring that the platform functions as a visual portfolio, but also as an exclusive licensing tool, was key to generating real value for the client.
  • Cohesion between storytelling, usability, and aesthetics: elements such as the use of geographic coordinates, subtle topographic patterns, and understated typography served to build an elegant identity with character, without overloading the experience.

    • This project allowed me not only to apply key UX/UI principles, but also to explore design as a tool to amplify a unique creative vision. Latitude 90° is more than an image gallery: it is a window into extreme territories, and the design had to live up to that message.
